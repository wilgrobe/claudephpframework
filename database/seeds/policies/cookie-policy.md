## 1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that a website stores in your browser when
you visit. They let the site remember information about your visit (such
as that you are signed in, or what theme you've chosen) so it can work
properly and provide a better experience the next time you return.

We also use related technologies that behave similarly to cookies — for
example, browser local storage, session storage, and (where applicable)
small tracking pixels. References to "cookies" in this Policy include
those technologies where they perform a similar function.

which of them are active.

### 2.1 Strictly necessary

## 3. How you control cookies

### 3.1 Through our consent banner

When you first visit the Service, we show a banner asking which non-
essential categories you would like to accept. You can choose **Accept
all**, **Reject all**, or open **Customize** to toggle each category
individually. Your choice is recorded with a timestamp and applies on
every subsequent visit from the same browser until you clear it or we
publish a new version of this policy.

You can change your choice at any time from the **Cookie preferences**
link in the site footer (or wherever else the cookie-reopen control is
exposed on this Service). Changing your choice takes effect immediately
on the next page load.

### 3.2 Through your browser

You can also configure your browser to block cookies, delete existing
cookies, or warn you before a cookie is stored. The exact controls vary
by browser; check your browser's "Settings" or "Preferences" menu for
"Privacy" or "Cookies" sections. Note that blocking strictly necessary
cookies will break authentication and most interactive features of the
Service.

### 3.3 Global Privacy Control

If your browser sends the `Sec-GPC: 1` header (the "Global Privacy
Control" signal), we treat that as an opt-out from any cookies that
would constitute a "sale" or "sharing" of personal information under
applicable law. You can read more about Global Privacy Control at
[https://globalprivacycontrol.org](https://globalprivacycontrol.org).

## 6. Do Not Track

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. There is no industry-
standard interpretation of this signal. We respond to the **Global
Privacy Control** signal (described above) instead, which has clearer
legal status under California's privacy laws and is honoured as an
opt-out from "sale" or "sharing" of personal information.
